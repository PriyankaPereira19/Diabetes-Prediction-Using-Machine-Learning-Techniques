Understanding Diabetes

Type 1 Diabetes

Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the body's immune system attacks insulin-producing cells in the pancreas. Causes of Type 1 diabetes are still being researched, but genetic factors and environmental triggers play a role.

Symptoms:

  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Extreme hunger
  • Weight loss
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision

Type 2 Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes occurs when the body becomes resistant to insulin or when the pancreas fails to produce enough insulin. It's often associated with obesity, physical inactivity, and poor diet.

Causes:

  • Genetics
  • Obesity
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Unhealthy diet

Symptoms:

  • Increased thirst and urination
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow healing of wounds
  • Numbness or tingling in hands or feet

Type 3 Diabetes

Type 3 diabetes is a proposed form of diabetes that links insulin resistance in the brain to Alzheimer's disease. While it’s still being studied, the connection suggests that Alzheimer’s could be another form of diabetes.

Causes:

  • Insulin resistance in the brain
  • Genetic factors
  • Lifestyle factors similar to those in Type 2 diabetes

Symptoms:

  • Memory loss
  • Difficulty completing familiar tasks
  • Confusion with time or place
  • Changes in mood or personality
